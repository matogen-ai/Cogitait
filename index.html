    Debt Collection Analytics
    Expand

    Hide

    COGITAIT partnered with a major telecom client to enhance their debt collection strategy through advanced analytics. Faced with the dual challenges of improving collection rates and retaining customers, the client collaborated with COGITAIT to leverage intelligent customer insights.
    The COGITAIT data science team conducted a comprehensive collections diagnostic and supported the client’s efforts in managing delinquent accounts. By employing advanced data wrangling, modelling, and alternative data sources, they optimized Right Party Contact (RPC) strategies, improving contact with delinquent customers and addressing issues like network dormancy. Additionally, they adjusted service packages to align with customers' current affordability profiles.
    This innovative approach not only increased collection rates but also significantly improved customer retention.

    Debt Collection Advanced Analytics

    The COGITAIT data science team undertook a comprehensive collections diagnostic, in addition to supporting the client’s collections team with managing the ‘run down’ book. COGITAIT harnessed the foundational components, Right Party Contact (RPC), gleaned from advanced data wrangling and modelling, as well as the client insights enriched by alternative data sources and application of new variables created from Credit Bureau data. Right Party Contact enables the client to get hold of delinquent customers, overcoming the challenges of contactability and network dormancy, whilst the advanced customer analytics made it possible to adjust the service component of the customer’s package downwards to match their current affordability profile.

    This innovative and flexible approach to debt collection analytics simultaneously increases collection, as well as customer retention rates.

  • Alternative Data Credit Scoring Expand

    Hide

    Traditionally, financial services have relied on credit bureau data to assess credit risk, focusing on past credit activities. In contrast, alternative data, which includes non-credit-related information like habits, preferences, and behavior, offers deeper insights into a customer's character. Importantly, this data must be from sources that cannot be manipulated by the individual. According to Experian, 65% of lenders already use alternative data in their lending decisions.

    Substitute Scorecards

    For a COGITAIT financial services client, traditional credit bureau data proved insufficient, particularly in key portfolios. To address this, COGITAIT developed substitute scorecards using alternative data from purchasing records within the client's data warehouse. By creatively compensating for missing information and analyzing purchasing data, COGITAIT identified attributes with strong predictive power. These insights were used to build new features and predictive models, resulting in scorecards that outperformed those based on traditional credit data.

    Marketing and Compliance

    This alternative data scoring framework will be continually used to inform ethical marketing strategies, mitigate risks like over-indebtedness, and enhance product offerings. Ultimately, the goal is to deepen the understanding of consumers, improve model accuracy, and strengthen compliance efforts.

  • Oncology Data Modelling Expand

    Oncology Data Modelling Expand

    Hide

    COGITAIT undertook oncology data modelling for an international client as part of a health sector Big Data project.

    In this project, data was extracted from a large, publicly available inpatient healthcare database in the United States. COGITAIT used this data to model outcomes for breast cancer patients, determining the most probable results based on patient attributes and guiding intervention decisions, such as whether to administer neoadjuvant chemotherapy or proceed with surgery. The study also explored predicting tumor downstaging and the level of lymphovascular invasion after chemotherapy, providing critical insights for personalized treatment plans.

  • Advanced Analytics for Orthopaedic Procedures Expand

    Advanced Analytics for Orthopaedic Procedures Expand

    Hide

    COGITAIT assisted a US healthcare client by applying advanced analytics to data on dismineral disease, orthopaedic procedures, and adverse events.

    Leveraging their expertise in healthcare analytics, COGITAIT investigated outcomes and risk factors for patients with dismineral diseases undergoing procedures like hip and knee arthroplasty and spinal fusion. Analyzing millions of records from a national database (2008–2014), the team used ICD9 codes to filter relevant data and employed SAS software to organize and analyze the dataset.
    The primary focus was on the incidence of mortality, where COGITAIT identified specific combinations of demographics, mineral deficiencies, and procedures that most closely correlated with post-procedural mortality, providing critical insights into patient risk factors and outcomes.

    LOYALTY: Personalized Customer Campaigns Expand

    Hide

    Partnering with one of the world’s largest Quick Service Restaurant organizations, we achieved a 48% increase in revenue, a 38% rise in transaction values, and a 43% boost in purchases per customer.

    In today’s competitive landscape, where customer loyalty is constantly tested by price sensitivity and competition, businesses need to connect with customers on a personal level. At COGITAIT, we provide Campaign as a Service to loyalty-driven industries, including QSR, retail, and hospitality.

    Our global QSR client approached us to enhance their marketing campaigns' effectiveness. We conducted an extensive segmentation exercise, analysing and modelling data using our proprietary AI and machine learning tools. This involved integrating customer behaviour with historical purchase data to develop two symbiotic models.

    The first, a Responder Model, predicted customers' likelihood to make a purchase. The second, a Recommender Model, targeted specific customer segments with tailored offers based on product purchase clusters. Additionally, it re-engaged dormant customers with value-driven offers to reduce churn. These models were combined using an AI algorithm that adjusted in real-time.

    The results speak for themselves

    • Transaction values grew by 38%
    • Loyalty transactions surged by 73%
